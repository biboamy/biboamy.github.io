Project

Music auto-tagging

Collaborators: KKBOX research team
Advisor: Dr. Yi-Hsuan Yang

The music auto-tagging project can be divided into three sub-projects: emotion tagging, genre tagging, and instrument recognition.

Instrument Recognition focuses on polyphonic frame-level instrument tagging, which means the model will predict the presence of instrument in each time step, so that we can know the starting time and ending time of each instrument. Besides, we also study the influence of pitch information in instrument recognition task by strengthen the harmonic structure. The model is trained with MusicNet Dataset. Please visit our demo page for further details.

Second stage of this project is to provide large scale training data. We proposed another join-learning model for training large data. The model is trained with Musescore Dataset, a new dataset containing polyphonic audio and midi pairs. (Result Demo)

Music Genre Tagging focuses on music genre and subgenre taggings. For example, given a song from Charlie Parker, the model will tell us its genre is jazz and its subgenre is Swing. We trained the model on the mix of KKBOX private dataset and Million Song Dataset(MSD). Please visit our demo page for more details.

Music Emotion Tagging is part of the project to generate music according to user's emotion. We train a deep learning model for emotion tagging on audio domain, and transfer the tags to MIDI domain by using Lakh MIDI Dataset(LMD). MIDI audio pair. The MIDI emotion tags will be used as a condition to generate music.

Music Streaming

Music streaming is the extension application of the instrument recognition project. Since the model has already learned the pitch and instrument information, it can further predict which instrument is playing the note in each time step. The streaming result can also extend to msic auto-transcription.
