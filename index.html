Project

Music auto-tagging

Collaborators: Machine Learning Research Team, KKBOX Inc
Advisor: Dr. Yi-Hsuan Yang

Music auto-tagging project can be divided into three sub-projects: instrument recognition, genre tagging, and emotion tagging.

Instrument Recognition focuses on polyphonic frame-level instrument tagging. The model will predict the presence of instruments in each time step so that we can know the starting time and ending time of each instrument. We proposed to use pitch to strengthen the harmonic structure in polyphonic music, which can also benefit instrument recognition result. The model is trained with MusicNet Dataset. Please visit our demo page for further details.

The second stage of this project is to increase instrument categories. We provide a large-scale dataset, Musescore Dataset, to train the model. Musescore Dataset contains over 330,000 pieces of songs with their audio and MIDI pairs. We also introduced a multitask model for training this large-scale dataset. Please visit our demo page or Github to see the result.

Music Genre Tagging focuses on music genre and subgenre taggings. For example, given a song from Charlie Parker, except for telling us the song belongs to jazz, the model will also tell us it belongs to Swing and Bebop. We trained the model on the mix of KKBOX private dataset and Million Song Dataset(MSD). Please visit our project page for more details.

Music Emotion Tagging is part of the project for conditional music generation. We train a deep learning model by private dataset for emotion tagging on the audio domain and transfer the tags to MIDI domain by using Lakh MIDI Dataset(LMD), which contains large-scale MIDI audio pairs. The MIDI emotion tags will be used as a condition to generate music.

Multi-pitch Streaming

Advisor: Dr. Yi-Hsuan Yang

Music streaming is the extension application of the instrument recognition project. We proposed a multitask learning model for predicting pitch, instrument, and pianorolls at the same time. The pianorolls prediction can also extend to multi-pitch streaming, meaning our model is able to predict which instrument is playing the note in each time step. Please see our paper or Github page for more details.

Music Disentanglement

Advisor: Dr. Yi-Hsuan Yang

In music disentanglement project, we proposed a deep learning algorithm for learning timbre and pitch representation, two fundamental features in music. The learned representations can be used in many applications, such as pitch and timbre classification, AI auto-composing, and music editing. Please visit our paper for more details.
